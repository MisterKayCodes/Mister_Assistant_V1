# Chapter 7: The Master of Coins

Next to the Keeper of Hours sits **The Master of Coins (Repository Spending)**. He is a simpler man, but very precise. He only cares about **Wealth and Value**.

When the user speaks of spending, The Master of Coins opens the **Golden Ledger** (The Spending Table). He assumes that unless you are a king, you are using the commoners' bank: **PalmPay**.

```python
    def log_spending(self, user_id, amount, category, date=None, payment_method="PalmPay"):
        if not date:
            date = datetime.now().strftime("%Y-%m-%d") # Today!
        cursor = self.conn.cursor()
        cursor.execute(
            "INSERT INTO spending (user_id, amount, category, date, payment_method) VALUES (?, ?, ?, ?, ?)",
            (user_id, amount, category, date, payment_method)
        )
        self.conn.commit()
```

He doesn't ask questions. He only records the truth of the sacrifice. ₦1000 for bread, ₦5000 for fuel. Every coin is tracked, and every coin is committed to the eternal stone of the database. He is the guardian of your prosperity.
